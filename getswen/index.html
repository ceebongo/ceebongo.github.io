What
Have you ever wondered how there are so many 'free' sites that bring you the news?
How do these sites make money? What is their product?
YOU are the Product!
Simply go to your favorite news sites, and let the filter do it's thing. If there are articles that make it through the filter, you will see a list of links from that site. When you click on one of those links, you are shown the article, from the site. Clicking on any other link on the site takes you back to the Swen article page.
Use the SWEN news filter to only show you what is really important. You can use the slider to control how much or how little news you see. Our algorithm gathers news events every fifteen minutes and ranks them based on global importance. The SWEN filter kicks in on most global news sites, and only shows links to articles that match your filter setting.
Why
The news sucks. It's a constant drumbeat of 'Breaking News' that is neither breaking nor news - it's mostly garbage. Do we really need an endless stream of drivel about inane celebrities or the day to day horse shit that passes for politics?
Why do news sites do this? What is the motivation? They have to, because, advertising. They need a constant 'look at this flashy thing' gimmick so you visit the site often. The more you visit, the more ad revenue they make. It is a self enforcing feedback loop for all the players involved. Except, in this game, only you are the loser. The news site is happy to sell you 'news', and the advertiser is happy to sell you whatever junk they are peddling.
For an eloquent and beautifully reasoned argument, we highly recommend reading this excellent article by Mark Manson.
Our main focus is legitimate news sites, but we also have switches to block Social Media and Fake News sites. These sites provide no social value.
How
Our algorithm gathers news events every fifteen minutes and ranks them based on global importance. We get data on news events and articles from around thousands of news sites around the world. Every 15 minutes, we update our database of news articles, and we only store 7 days worth of articles. Using a consensus-type algorithm, we rank the importance of events. If more news articles are talking about an event, it rises up in the rankings. Recency of articles also increases their rank.
NOTE FOR PUBLISHERS. SWEN does NOT visit, crawl, or store content from your site. We do not condone circumvention of any paywalls or other revenue mechanism on your site. Individual users are responsible for purchasing content (if applicable) from your site.
